New Septic System Installation in Miami, FL

Miami-Dade County Ordinance 22-83 effective January 1, 2023 prohibits conventional Type 1 septic systems for new installations, requiring Type 2, Type 3, or Type 4 systems instead. New septic system installation in Miami-Dade typically costs $8,000-$25,000 depending on system type, size, site conditions, and installation complexity.

Installation process requires site evaluation, soil testing, permit approval from DERM, professional design, licensed contractor installation, and final inspection before use. New systems must maintain minimum 100 feet from surface water bodies, 50 feet from property lines, and drainfield bottom must be 36 inches above wet season high water table.

Professional installation by licensed contractors ensures compliance with Miami-Dade County requirements and protects groundwater in sensitive environmental conditions. All new installations require prior written approval from Miami-Dade DERM Director, comprehensive documentation, and proper system design for site conditions.

Installation process step-by-step

New septic system installation follows structured process ensuring compliance and proper function:

Step 1: Site evaluation and soil testing

Licensed professional conducts comprehensive site evaluation including soil percolation testing to measure how quickly water flows through native soil, soil composition analysis to identify soil type and properties, property boundary assessment to verify setback compliance, water well identification to ensure minimum separation distances, surface water mapping to identify proximity to water bodies, topographic analysis to evaluate elevation and drainage, and environmental sensitivity evaluation. Site evaluation determines appropriate system type and design.

Step 2: System design and planning

Based on site evaluation results, professional engineer or qualified designer creates system design meeting Miami-Dade County requirements. Design includes tank size and type selection, drainfield sizing and layout, treatment system specifications, elevation and setback compliance, and construction specifications. Design must meet Type 2, Type 3, or Type 4 requirements depending on site conditions.

Step 3: Permit application

Submit required documentation to Miami-Dade DERM including site plan showing property boundaries and system location, building plan showing proposed construction, site evaluation results including soil testing data, system design specifications, and permit application forms. Prior written approval from DERM Director is required before installation can begin.

Step 4: Permit approval

DERM reviews application for compliance with regulations, verifies setback requirements, evaluates system design appropriateness, and issues approval or requests modifications. Review process typically takes several weeks to months depending on application completeness and complexity.

Step 5: Professional installation

Licensed contractor performs installation including excavation for tank and drainfield, tank placement and leveling, drainfield construction and pipe installation, backfilling and soil compaction, and connection to building plumbing. Installation must follow approved design specifications exactly.

Step 6: Inspection and approval

After installation completion, state inspector conducts final inspection to verify compliance with approved design, check proper installation practices, ensure setback requirements are met, and approve system for use. System must pass inspection before use.

Step 7: System registration

Register completed system with DERM as required by county ordinance. Registration provides system documentation for future reference and compliance tracking.

Following proper installation process ensures system function, environmental protection, and regulatory compliance. Professional contractors manage entire process efficiently.

System type requirements

Miami-Dade County requires specific system types for new installations:

Type 2 systems:
  • Meet secondary treatment standards
  • Suitable for single-family homes or duplexes
  • Generate less than 500 gallons per day
  • Located at least 1,000 feet from surface water bodies
  • Cost $8,000-$15,000 typically
Type 3 systems:
  • Meet advanced secondary treatment standards
  • For multifamily residences and other uses
  • Generate up to 1,000 gallons per day
  • Located at least 1,000 feet from surface water bodies
  • Cost $12,000-$20,000 typically
Type 4 systems:
  • Meet Florida Keys Nutrient Reduction Treatment Standards
  • Required when Type 2 or Type 3 criteria not met
  • Provide enhanced nutrient reduction
  • For sensitive environmental areas
  • Cost $15,000-$30,000 typically
Type 1 systems:

Conventional Type 1 systems are prohibited for new installations effective January 1, 2023. Only Type 2, Type 3, or Type 4 systems are allowed for new installations or complete replacements. Existing Type 1 systems can continue operating but must meet maintenance requirements.

System selection:

System type selection depends on property usage, wastewater volume, proximity to surface water, environmental sensitivity, and site conditions. Professional contractors and engineers evaluate site conditions and recommend appropriate system type meeting requirements.

Selecting appropriate system type ensures compliance with Miami-Dade County regulations and environmental protection. Professional evaluation determines correct system for each property.

Setback and design requirements

New septic systems must meet specific setback and design requirements:

Setback distances:
  • Minimum 100 feet from any surface water body
  • Minimum 50 feet from property lines (where public water unavailable)
  • Required distances protect water quality
  • Non-compliance prevents installation
Drainfield elevation:
  • Drainfield bottom must be 36 inches above wet season high water table
  • Prevents groundwater contamination
  • Affects system design and cost
  • Elevated drainfields may be required
Additional requirements:
  • Systems cannot be under buildings
  • Five feet minimum from mobile home walls
  • Five feet minimum from building foundations
  • 75 feet from annual flood lines of surface water
Design standards:
  • Must meet treatment standards for system type
  • Proper tank sizing for property usage
  • Adequate drainfield sizing for soil conditions
  • Compliance with all Miami-Dade regulations

Meeting setback and design requirements ensures environmental protection and regulatory compliance. Professional contractors understand requirements and ensure installations meet all standards.
