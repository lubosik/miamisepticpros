Expansion process step-by-step

Expansion follows structured process ensuring proper capacity addition:

Step 1: System evaluation

Professional evaluation assesses current system capacity, determines increased needs, evaluates existing component condition, identifies expansion options, and calculates required capacity increase. Evaluation provides foundation for expansion planning.

Step 2: Site assessment

Site assessment evaluates available space for expansion, tests soil conditions in expansion area, checks elevation and water table, verifies setback compliance, and determines best expansion approach. Assessment ensures expansion feasible.

Step 3: Expansion design

Engineer designs expansion meeting capacity needs, determines drainfield expansion requirements, designs new components meeting current standards, prepares permit drawings, and specifies installation requirements. Design ensures compliance and proper function.

Step 4: Permit application

Submit permit application to DERM including expansion plans, obtain required approvals, secure building department permit if needed, and complete all regulatory requirements. Permits required before expansion work begins.

Step 5: Drainfield expansion

Excavate area for additional drainfield lines or new drainfield section, install additional distribution lines, connect to existing drainfield, ensure proper slope and installation, and complete drainfield expansion. Drainfield expansion adds capacity needed.

Step 6: System connection

Connect expanded drainfield to existing system, ensure proper flow distribution, install any needed distribution boxes, verify connections are secure, and test system flow. Connections must ensure even distribution.

Step 7: System testing

Test expanded system for proper function, verify capacity increase meets needs, check flow distribution, ensure no leaks or issues, and confirm system operating properly. Testing verifies expansion success.

Step 8: Final inspection

DERM inspector conducts final inspection verifying expansion meets approved plans, checking installation quality, ensuring compliance with standards, and approving system for use. System must pass inspection before becoming fully operational.

Following proper expansion process ensures compliance, proper function, and capacity increase. Professional contractors manage entire process meeting all requirements.
