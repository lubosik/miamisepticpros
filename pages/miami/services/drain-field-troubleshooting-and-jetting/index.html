How drain field jetting works

Hydro jetting process follows structured steps:

Step 1: System access

Technicians access drain field lines through cleanout ports or septic tank connections. Access points located and prepared. Equipment positioned for service. Safety measures implemented.

Step 2: Diagnostic assessment

Video inspection conducted to assess pipe condition, identify blockages, locate root intrusion, evaluate pipe damage, and determine jetting approach. Diagnostics guide jetting process.

Step 3: High-pressure jetting

High-pressure water streams directed through drain field lines, removing blockages and debris, cutting through roots, cleaning pipe walls, and restoring flow capacity. Pressure adjusted for pipe condition.

Step 4: Debris removal

Flushed debris removed from system, ensuring complete cleaning, verifying flow restoration, and confirming system function. Debris disposal handled properly.

Step 5: Verification

System tested for proper function, flow verified, blockages confirmed removed, and system operating normally. Final inspection confirms success.
