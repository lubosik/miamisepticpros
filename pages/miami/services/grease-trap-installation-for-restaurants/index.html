What is a grease trap?

Grease traps (also called grease interceptors) are plumbing devices that intercept fats, oils, greases (FOG), and solid food waste before they enter the municipal wastewater system. They function as containment boxes that wastewater flows through before entering the drainage system.

How grease traps work:
  • Wastewater from sinks, dishwashers, and floor drains flows into trap
  • System separates materials by density
  • Solids settle to bottom of container
  • Greases and oils float to surface
  • Cleaner water passes through to sewer system
Why restaurants need grease traps:
  • Restaurants produce substantially more FOG than residential kitchens
  • Continuous food preparation creates large volumes of greasy wastewater
  • Grease solidifies when cooled and can block drain pipes
  • Prevents sewer backups and environmental pollution
  • Legal requirement in most jurisdictions including Miami-Dade
  • Required for business license and health department approval
Benefits:
  • Prevents pipe blockages and sewer backups
  • Protects municipal sewer systems
  • Environmental protection
  • Compliance with legal requirements
  • Prevents fines and penalties
  • Maintains restaurant operations

Grease traps are essential for restaurants to protect plumbing systems and comply with Miami-Dade County requirements. Proper installation and maintenance ensure effective operation.
