What is an advanced treatment unit?

Advanced Treatment Units (ATUs) are aerobic wastewater treatment systems that use oxygen to break down septic waste more efficiently than conventional anaerobic septic tanks. ATUs inject air into wastewater through mechanical blowers or compressors to create oxygen-rich environment supporting specialized aerobic bacteria capable of more thorough waste breakdown.

How ATUs work:
  • Wastewater enters aeration chamber
  • Air is injected through blowers or compressors
  • Oxygen supports aerobic bacteria growth
  • Bacteria digest organic matter more thoroughly
  • Solids and bacteria settle in clarification chamber
  • Some settled material recirculated for additional treatment
  • High-quality effluent discharged to drainfield
Treatment quality:
  • Less than 30 mg/L biochemical oxygen demand (BOD)
  • Less than 25 mg/L total suspended solids (TSS)
  • Less than 10,000 CFU/100ml fecal coliform bacteria
  • Significantly clearer effluent than conventional systems
  • Superior pathogen reduction
Key differences from conventional:
  • Oxygen-based treatment vs anaerobic
  • Mechanical air injection required
  • Superior treatment quality
  • Smaller drainfield possible
  • More maintenance required

ATUs provide superior wastewater treatment meeting stricter environmental standards (EPA). Professional installation ensures proper mechanical function and treatment effectiveness.

Types of ATU systems

ATUs are available in three primary operational designs:

Suspended-Growth Systems:
  • Bacteria suspended in wastewater
  • Air forcefully mixed using blowers
  • Settlement chamber for solids separation
  • Settled bacteria returned to aeration chamber
  • Most common ATU type
Fixed-Film Systems:
  • Porous surface material for bacterial biofilm
  • Media alternately immersed and exposed
  • Biofilm digests waste during contact
  • Settlement chamber for solids separation
  • Efficient treatment with media support
Sequencing Batch Reactors:
  • Single chamber for all processes
  • Cyclical treatment process
  • Air bubbled during decomposition
  • Air shut off during settling
  • Space-efficient design

System type selection depends on property needs, space constraints, and treatment requirements (Florida DOH). Professional engineers recommend appropriate ATU type based on specific conditions.

Installation process step-by-step

ATU installation follows structured process ensuring proper function:

Step 1: Site evaluation and design

Professional evaluation assesses site conditions, determines if ATU appropriate, selects appropriate system type, designs system meeting Type 3 or Type 4 requirements, and prepares installation specifications. Evaluation provides foundation for installation planning.

Step 2: Permit acquisition

Apply for DERM permit including ATU specifications, obtain operating permit approval, secure building department permit, and complete all required approvals. ATU installations require operating permits for continued compliance.

Step 3: Excavation and preparation

Excavate area for ATU unit and drainfield, prepare base for unit installation, ensure proper positioning, and prepare site for mechanical components. Excavation must accommodate unit size and installation requirements.

Step 4: ATU unit installation

Install ATU unit in prepared location, position unit properly, ensure level installation, connect inlet and outlet pipes, and secure unit in place. Unit installation requires precision for proper function.

Step 5: Mechanical component installation

Install air blowers or compressors, connect pumps and timers, install control systems, ensure proper electrical connections, and verify mechanical function. Mechanical components essential for ATU operation.

Step 6: Electrical system installation

Install electrical service to ATU, connect control panels, ensure proper power supply, install backup power if required, and verify electrical safety. Electrical systems must meet code requirements.

Step 7: Drainfield installation

Install smaller drainfield sized for ATU effluent quality, connect to ATU outlet, ensure proper drainage, and complete drainfield installation. ATU effluent quality allows smaller drainfield than conventional systems.

Step 8: System startup and testing

Start up mechanical components, test air injection systems, verify treatment function, test effluent quality, and ensure proper operation. System startup ensures proper function before final approval.

Step 9: Final inspection

DERM inspector conducts final inspection verifying compliance with approved plans, checking mechanical components, ensuring proper function, and approving system for operation. System must pass inspection before becoming operational.

Following proper installation process ensures compliance, proper function, and long-term reliability (Florida DOH). Professional contractors manage entire process meeting all requirements.
